Carolina Chávez

Aimé Cuéllar is a student of M. Sc. in Nanotechnology at Instituto Tecnológico y de Estudios Superiores de Monterrey (I.T.E.S.M.). She joined the Cellular Engineering and Bioreaction Research Group since 2016. The thesis project is to Develop an Oral Bioadhesive for Surgery Wounds. She received her degree as Dental Surgeon from Universidad Autónoma de Nuevo León (U.A.N.L., 2014). In 2017, she made a research stay in Cellular Engineering and Bioreaction Research Group, support in Biomaterials workshop in Nucleum Science and Technology Congress. Her laboratory skills are Hydrogels and Alginate Printing and mammalian and bacteria culture, as well, her clinical skills are dental rehabilitation, preparation and management of alginate as a dental printing material, use of composite as dental restorations and extraction and post operatory dental treatment.
